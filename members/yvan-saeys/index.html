Yvan Saeys

Yvan Saeys group leader

I graduated as a computer scientist from Ghent University in 2000, and obtained my PhD in computer science at the Bioinformatics group of the Flemish Institute for Biotechnology (VIB) and Ghent University. After spending time abroad at the University of the Basque Country (Spain) and the Universit√© Claude Bernard (Lyon 1, France), I established the DAMBI research group at the Inflammation Research Centre (IRC), focusing on the development and application of new data mining and machine learning techniques for biomedicine.

My research focuses on the interplay between computer science and biology and medicine. I am particularly fascinated by the tailored design of state-of-the-art computational techniques for specific biological or medical questions.

Recurring themes in my research include the design and application of optimization methods and data mining techniques to analyze large amounts of biological data in an automated and high-throughput way. This way, new biological insights and hypotheses can be generated that can be further investigated in detail within the framework of collaborations with specific wet lab groups that are domain experts in the processes being investigated.

I am also actively involved in the data mining and machine learning research communities where I advocate new challenges for data mining and machine learning research motivated by novel learning questions or data types generated in the field of the life sciences.
